My Story

My Journey

2004

Lawrence first fell in love with technology as a young boy and started tinkering with computers, building his own at the age of 12.

2010

After graduating from Stuyvesant High School, he spent a summer in Taiwan learning how to repair broken iPhones.
He started his Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering at UC Berkeley.

2011

Applying his new skill, Lawrence started his own iPhone Repair Service based out of his own apartment, servicing over 60 customers.
He started an internship at Lawrence Berkeley Lab as IT support for the Math Building.

2012

Pursuing a career in Civil Engineering, Lawrence did a summer internship at the San Francisco Department of Public Works.

2013

With credits from 10 AP Tests, Lawrence graduates UC Berkeley with a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering a full year early.
He starts working in the Civil Engineering field as an Assistant Project Manager, specializing in heavy steel work.

2014

After working a year and a half in the construction industry, not feeling very challenged, Lawrence decides to look for other prospects.
He starts working at Accenture as a Technology Consultant in Los Angeles, CA.

2015

Lawrence starts his first project at Accenture as a Training and Implementation Analyst.
He is given his own digital project with a small startup, inciting his love for digital work of design, development and user research. His work results in three more contracts from the client.

2016

Lawrence completes his digital projects with great customer satisfaction.
He is given and completes a subsequent digital business development project with a large media corporation.
Deciding to focus more on digital work, Lawrence leaves Accenture when a former Manager offers a position at a new startup.
Lawrence begins working at Flyosk as the Director of Operations, working on all aspects of the product including marketing, user experience design, front-end development, graphic design, product management, product strategy, investor relations, and metrics analysis.
He starts helping his friend's new startup, Horizon, design a new logo, business card, and website and begins development.

2017

He completes development of the company site, Horizon.
As funding for the startup dries up, Lawrence is beginning to search for other prospects in the tech world to continue his love of digital product development and continue developing his skills.
